In June 1838, Georgetown president and Jesuit priest Fr. Thomas Mulledy agreed to sell 272 men, women, and children. They were sold to Henry Johnson, a member of the House of Representatives, and Jesse Beatty, a landowner in Louisiana. The sale was worth about $3.3 million in today's dollars.

In Dec. 1838, a group of the slaves were transported from Alexandria to New Orleans on the ship manifest of the Katharine Jackson. The cargo included dozens of children, among them infants as young as 2 months old. They were shipped by Robert A. Windsor of Alexandria and consigned to Lambeth and Thompson of New Orleans.

In March 1848, Reverend James Van de Velde lamented that the religious instruction of the slaves sold to Henry Johnson has been neglected. He urges Jesuit leaders to send money for the construction of a church that would "provide for the salvation of those poor people, who are now utterly neglected."
